Data centers do a lot of [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookups. Every company has a [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) and spends lots of time and money looking stuff up in their database.

If we could make [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookups more efficient, [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) centers would save a lot of money on their electricity bills â perhaps $100 million per year.

Appa Apps Ltd Inc. is prototyping a specialized [Silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon) [chip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) to perform [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookups 10 times more efficiently and 10 times faster than conventional computers can.

This is not a new idea.

Today, everyone has a mobile phone with a graphics processing unit (GPU) implemented in [Silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon) rather than [software](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software) because the original software-only versions were too slow, and the mobile phone was the size of a brick. The same applies to every personal computer.

Bitcoin mining used to be done in just [software](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software). You could easily mine a few bitcoins every day with a generic notebook [computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer). Now, you need to use a specialized Bitcoin miner that does most of that processing in [Silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon) [chips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) rather than software.

Implementing [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) [software](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software) in [Silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon) will [help](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_help) [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) center operators be more competitive by reducing their cost per [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookup.

Appa Apps has just succeeded in synthesizing the [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) algorithm, ready for placement on a field-programmable [gate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logic_gate) [array](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamic_array) (FPGA).

We are going to compare the performance of our specialized implementation of the [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) algorithm with the performance of a generic [computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer) running the same algorithm.

If our specialized [chip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) can do [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookups significantly faster using less power than the generic computers currently used by [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) center operators, then we are going to send them copies of the [chip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) so that they can see for themselves that they are wasting money on their electricity bills unless they buy our chips.
