The problem

Data centers often use one hundred times as much power as other businesses according to the Wall Street Journal, and this percentage is increasing year by year.

That is more than the total amount of electricity consumed by these major global cities:

As a consequence, [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) center operators like to locate their [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) centers close to reliable supplies of cheap electricity.

For example, Gmail is located near the Boneville Dam in The Cascades, Oregon.

Global electricity production currently amounts to $2.1 trillion annually â equivalent to the GDP of Italy.

Data centers do a lot of [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookups. Every company has a [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) and every company spends lots of time and money looking stuff up in their [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) quickly for their demanding customers.

The Solution

If we could make [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookups more efficient, [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) centers would save a lot of money on their electricity bills â perhaps $100 million per year.

Appa Apps Ltd Inc. is prototyping a specialized [Silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon) [chip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) to perform [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookups using 10 times less electricity while doing the look up 10 times faster than generic computers can.

This is not a new idea.

Today, everyone has a mobile phone with a graphics processing unit (GPU) implemented in [Silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon) rather than [software](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software) because the original software-only versions were too slow while the mobile phone was the size of a brick. Every personal [computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer) has a GPU now-a-days for the same reason.

Bitcoin mining used to be done in just [software](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software). At one time you could easily mine a few bitcoins every day with a generic notebook [computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer). Now, you need to use a [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) center full of specialized Bitcoin miners that do most of that processing in [Silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon) rather than in software.

Implementing [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) [software](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software) effectively in [Silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon) will [help](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_help) [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) center operators be more competitive by reducing their cost per [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookup while speeding up such look-ups at the same time.

Appa Apps has just succeeded in synthesizing a Verilog [version](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_versioning) of the well known [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) algorithm normally written in the C programming language, ready for placement on an [field programmable [gate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logic_gate) array](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field-programmable_gate_array) (FPGA).

Roadmap

We are going to use a [field programmable [gate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logic_gate) array](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field-programmable_gate_array) mounted on a card to compare the performance of our specialized implementation of the [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) algorithm with the performance of a generic [computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer) running the same algorithm in conventional code.

If our specialized implementation can do [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) lookups significantly faster using less power than the generic implementation, then we are going to send [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) center operators copies of the [field programmable [gate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logic_gate) array](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field-programmable_gate_array) on a card so that they can plug it in and see for themselves that they are currently wasting money, needlessly, on their electricity bills unless they use our design.

We need to raise $1M to buy [hardware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_electronics) to demonstrate our technology, [hardware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_electronics) engineers to implement our technology on an actual [field programmable [gate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logic_gate) array](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field-programmable_gate_array) connected to the real world via [Ethernet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethernet), and sales and marketing personnel to present our findings to the major [data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data) center operators with the goal of getting letters of intent from them indicating that they are interested in buying our chips.

If they are willing to sign letters of intent to buy such [chips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) when they become available, we will use those letters to drive a large Series A funding round and get into the business of saving the planet by reducing the electricity consumed by each [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database) while making a lot of money.

Why now?

  • Moore's law has come to an end so generic [chips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) are starting to give way to specialist [chips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit).
  • The AI boom is creating lots of spare fabrication capacity at the semiconductor factories that produce [chips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) because they thought that they would need a lot of capacity until DeepSeek proved otherwise by wiping $700 billion off value of Nvidia in one day.
  • AI makes it possible to explore the myriad [chip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit) design possibilities much more easily than ever before.
